Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a dish of eggs (and sometimes meat) that is poached in a spicy tomato sauce.

Ingredients
  • 3 tins of diced tomatoes
  • 200g beef or lamb mince
  • 2T cumin
  • 2T tumeric
  • 3T paprika (sweet or hot)
  • 3T butter or olive oil
  • 3 brown onions, diced
  • 2T minced garlic
  • 6 garlic cloves, diced
  • 2T soy sauce
  • 6 eggs
Method
  1. Heat the oil or butter in a large frying pan with high sides. You will need a pan with high sides to accomodate the sauce and eggs.
  2. Over medium heat, saute the onion gently, without letting it become crisp or burnt. If you can afford the time to caramelise it, even better.
  3. Add the garlic, and stir in to the onion, making sure not that it doesn't burn too.
  4. Combine the spices in a small bowl, and then add the onions. Stir until the mixture is fragrant.
  5. In a bowl, separate the mince with your hands, so it will cook more evenly and equally in the pan. Increase the heat to high, and add the mince to the pan.
  6. Brown the mince, stirring continuously. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, and add the tomatoes. Simmer gently for 40 minutes. If the sauce seems dry, or is sticking, add 2-3T of water at a time.
  8. Add the soy sauce, and stir to combine.
  9. Stir the sauce, and arrange the surface with a spoon so it is even and flat. One at a time, crack each egg onto the surface of the sauce. Work around the perimeter of the pan, and then into the centre, so the eggs will not combine together.
  10. Poach the eggs until the whites are firm. If this is taking too long, cover the pan with the lid for a few minutes, checking frequently to make sure the yolks are not overcooking.
  11. To serve, use a large ladle to scoop out the eggs with the sauce and meat.
  12. Shakshuka is best served with a side of rice or cous cous, and a simple green salad.
