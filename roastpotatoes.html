Roast Potatoes

Ingredients

Roast potatoes seem like an easy dish to make, but they are difficult to get perfectly right; crispy on the and outside, and fluffy on the inside. The key is to extract as much moisture as possible from the potatoes before they are roasted in the oven.

The quantities in the recipe will serve four people, as a side dish.

  • 1kg potatoes, I like Royal Blues
  • 0.5c oil, rice bran is a good choice, as it has a high smoke point
  • Salt and pepper, for seasoning
  • Fresh rosemary leaves (optional)
Method
  1. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees.
  2. Put the oil into a large roasting dish, and then put the roasting dish in the oven on the middle shelf.
  3. Peel the potatoes.
  4. Cut each potato into the size you would like to them to be. I cut mine into half, and then into half again.
  5. Place the potatoes in a large pot, and fill with cold water. Cover the pot with a lid, and put it on the stove over high heat.
  6. Eventually, the potatoes will come to the boil. Boil them for about 5-10 minutes, or until no resistance remains when they are prodded with a form.
  7. Drain the potatoes in a colander, and leave for a moment for the excess moisture to evaporate.
  8. While the potatoes are resting, put the pot you used for boiling the potatoes back on the stove. Turn the heat to medium.
  9. Put the potatoes back in the pot on the stove.
  10. Holding both handles, vigorously shake and turn the pot, so the surface of the potatoes is roughened. Put the pot back down on the heat for a moment, then repeat the shaking action one more.
  11. Remove the pot from the heat.
  12. Retrieve the roasting pan from the oven, and place on a heat proof surface.
  13. Facing the pot away from you, empty the potatoes into the roasting pan. The oil will be extremely hot, so position yourself away from where the oil could splatter.
  14. Using a spoon, distribute the potatoes in the roasting pan so they are arranged in a single layer.
  15. Season with salt and pepper.
  16. Bake in the oven for 40 minutes.
  17. The potatoes will be done when they are golden and crispy.
  18. If you like, the potatoes can be tossed with some fresh rosemary leaves just prior to serving. I find that this is preferable to adding the rosemary in at the oven stage, as the herb becomes burnt and sharp tasting.
